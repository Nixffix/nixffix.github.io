HALO

Halo is a tokenised sci fi strategy game and economic simulation on a galactic scale. Halo is the world’s first science fiction themed “4X” play-to-earn game involving combat, exploration, mining, developing planets, base building, token farming, and trading. All resources such as minerals, and structures such as ships and buildings are mined and created by the players themselves.

Halo provides intuitive gameplay intertwined with deep strategy, enabling absolute beginners and crypto veterans to earn real crypto exploring, mining, trading, building, and fighting in a vast galaxy of limitless possibilities.

Introducing HALO

Developing Planets & Generating Resources
Building structures on Planet and Asteroid NFTs is the core activity of the economic simulation. Buildings cost tokenised resources to build, and different buildings generate different resources daily. They are key to any strategy to maximise resource production and profits for players, providing a reliable income of tokens on a daily basis. For details of the buildings available please see the chapter Buildings [link to chapter] later in this whitepaper. Planets and Asteroids are NFT collections; players will need to own Planets and / or Asteroids to be able to build buildings on them and develop their worlds.

Lore

In the far future humanity had inexorably spread across the cosmos. This was a time of utopia and peace, trillions lived in paradise on a million worlds . There was no war: everyone lived for a thousand Earth years and pursued their highest calling. Beautiful works of art were created, indeed whole worlds were created as art, and humans were on the verge of discovering the secrets of existence itself. Sentient robots and uplifted apes, wolves, dinosaurs and monkeys did humanity’s bidding.

Then came the great collapse. No one was sure how it started, but everyone saw how it ended. All of humanity’s advanced tech became useless in an instant. Trillions perished from famine, disease, and the wars that erupted over the remaining rudimentary resources of each world.

A secretive group known as The Guardians had been protecting highly advanced AI tech for thousands of years. Many gave their lives for their sacred mission. Like a phoenix rising from the ashes, to try and restore order in the galaxy The Guardians created the Halo Nexus: a hyperintelligent AI to oversee all technology, all trade, and all resource distribution in the galaxy. Under the ever watchful and omniscient Halo Nexus AI, humanity and their uplifted ape brethren could begin again on their quest to regain their former glory. Will you help them?

PRESS RELEASE. Combining play-to-earn mechanics with base building, developing planets, trading, staking, missions, colonization, combat, and more, Halo offers players a strategic galactic experience with limitless earning potential
15 August, 2024, Location — Halo, is a play-to-earn sci-fi strategy game that focuses heavily on providing its players with a plethora of different ways to earn through the ecosystem’s 15 in-game currencies and the game’s main cryptocurrency, Halo. The game will launch in Q4, 2024, on both the Ethereum and Polygon networks.
What is Halo?
Halo is a tokenized, sci-fi strategy game set in deep space, with a huge galaxy of realistic planets, asteroids, dyson spheres, ringworlds, ships, and space structures for players to fully immerse themselves in. The game offers players several key gameplay loops, including: staking, planetary development, building structures, resource generation, ship building, missions, exploration, colonization, combat, and in-game trading. Players can utilize up to 15 in-game currencies in a multitude of different ways to forge their own unique path to wealth amongst the stars.
Exploring the Game
The more ambitious Halo explorers can engage in deep space exploration, combative gameplay in Contested Space, whilst extensively upgrading bases and other structures.
More casual players looking to earn passively can trade all of the in-game currencies, along with a multitude of in-game items, NFTs, and earn an APY from numerous staking options.
All Halo ships, resources, structures, and buildings are created by the players, and can be used to forge expansive space empires, mine exotic resources, or take on space colonization missions. Players can earn, mine, trade, or stake Halo’s 15 in-game currencies through playing the game, or using the in-game DEX.
Ways To Earn
Halo players can choose from a multitude of way to earn, including:
  • Developing planets and earning a reliable income of the 15 different tokens from different buildings and structures
  • Taking on daring, time-sensitive space missions
  • Exploring Contested Space
  • Trading & developing planets, asteroids, and other structures to sell P2P
  • Soft staking supported external NFTs + specialist NFT collections
  • Trade planets, Asteroids, Ships, & Crew NFTs via Halo Nexus NFT Marketplace
  • Trade Halo on MetaMask or Uniswap [ETH & MATIC]
  • Trade up to 15 in-game currencies on Halo’s native DEX
  • The Halo player referral program
  • Staking all Halo currencies via on-chain liquidity pools
Tokenomics
The Halo team is focused on making sure there are enough resources for further development of the game over a long period of time, creating an environment for stability and steady growth for both the game and the Halo community.
For this reason, there will be no Halo token sale. Instead, 80% of the total supply is locked into the game for players to earn, through various gameplay loops. This emergent economic structure is designed to benefit active players to have a key role in deciding the fate of the game. Players who wish to progress instantly can get a boost by purchasing Halo from DEXs or in the game.
Early Adopter Rewards
To ensure the 15 in-game currencies remain valuable, all rewards from buildings, missions, and soft staking will be gradually reduced over time. The first reward structure rebalancing will take place 90 days after launch, giving early adopters of Halo the highest rewards that will be available in the game.
NFTs
In Halo, Planets and Asteroids are player-owned NFTs with changing metadata that reflect players’ buildings, and customization of their worlds. In addition, there are two Halo NFT collections: Crew Member NFTs, and Specialist NFTs — each offering unique benefits for missions and resource generation for their holders. Halo will also support 17 current NFT collections including Aavegotchi, Galactic Apes, Galactic Apes Genesis, Star Wolvez, and many more.
Players can buy and trade Halo NFTs on the Halo Nexus NFT Marketplace. 10% of all ETH and Matic from all sales of planets, asteroids and crew NFT’s directly from the game will be used to purchase Halo and resource tokens to provide stability to the market. Halo will launch in Q4, 2024.

Planet and Asteroid NFTs — Unlock The Earning Potential of Halo
Planet and asteroid assets are at the core of the economic simulation of Halo, HALO. Players build buildings on the bedrock of their exotic planets to passively earn Halo, and the 14 other on-chain resources. It is the key way to maximize your income while building out your empire.
There are three different types of planets, and three different types of asteroids, with the rarest of each offering the most potential for rapid earnings. These NFTs also contain metadata about the number of buildings and the level of each building, making the more developed Planets and Asteroids rare, and more valuable on the NFT market.
Planet NFTs — The Foundation of Your Empire
Halo explorers need at least one planet to start building out their empires but have no limitations on expanding with multiple planets and asteroids. The more of each you own, the more building structures you can have and the more passive tokens you can earn.
Planets and Asteroids come in three types: common, uncommon, and rare, with more building slots the rarer the planet is. Planets with a great number of building slots accommodate more structures which generate passive Halo tokens and the other 14 on-chain resources.
At launch there will be 2,500 common planets, 1,500 uncommon planets, and 1,000 rare planets.
  • Common planets cost 0.05 ETH, and come with 10 building slots
  • Uncommon Planets Cost 0.07 ETH and come with 15 building slots
  • Rare Planets Cost 0.1 ETH and come with 20 building slots
Supercharge Your Economy with Asteroids
Asteroids can be used to subsidize your growing empire, with each offering additional building slots for increasing your token economy.
At launch there will be 2,500 common asteroids, 1,500 uncommon asteroids, and 1,000 rare asteroids.
  • Common asteroids cost 0.01 ETH, and come with 2 building slots
  • Uncommon asteroids Cost 0.02 ETH and come with 4 building slots
  • Rare asteroids Cost 0.03 ETH and come with 6 building slots
Upgrading Buildings
In the early game, explorers must use their planet’s building slots to build and upgrade structures to start generating passive tokens. It is a reliable form of daily passive income in Halo, that can also be increased by building more buildings, and upgrading existing buildings.
Important buildings like mines, barracks, citadels, factories, refineries, and arenas all help to generate Halo and other critical resources like HLMNR, HLORE, HLNRG, and HFOOD. Later on, building Megastructures like ringworlds and dyson spheres offer 5 additional buildings to grow your galactic empire into a powerful, self-sustaining economy.
Upgrade Costs & Output
Each building level costs 1.5x the previous level cost, with each level generating 1.5x the tokens of the previous level. Your Colony HQ is at the core of your empire and must be upgraded periodically, before you can upgrade your building levels. Building resource consumption of HLNRG and / or HFOOD also increases by 1.5x per building level.

NFTs play a vital role in the Halo ecosystem, coming in the form of 3 collections: Planets and Asteroids, Crew Members and Specialists.
Planet & Asteroid NFTs
When starting out, players will need Planet or Asteroid NFTs to build structures (like a Colony HQ or different token mines) upon. These structures passively generate daily resources in the form of the game’s 15 different currencies, including Halo token. These resources are vital to growing your empire from a lowly outpost to an indomitable mega-city.
Planets and Asteroids come in three sizes: common, uncommon, and rare, with more building slots the rarer the planet is.
These NFTs also contain metadata about the number of buildings and the level of each building, making the more developed Planets and Asteroids rare, and more valuable on the NFT market. All buildings can be upgraded to level 10 and generate more tokens at each level.
Planet and Asteroids NFTs can be purchased directly from the game, or from other players on the NFT marketplace. The game will launch with 5,000 Planet NFTs and 5,000 Asteroid NFTs.
Crew Member & Specialist NFTs
To undertake missions, your ships need to be staffed by Crew member NFTs. As a Halo explorer you can Increase your galactic reach with multiple crew members, with the rarest, “legendary” crew NFTs providing the ultimate reward levels for space missions. There will be common, uncommon, rare, and legendary crew member NFTs available with the common NFTs offering a very affordable way to boost your mission rewards.
Players can also man ships with Specialists NFTs to take advantage of extra bonuses for different mission varieties and building structures. This allows for exciting opportunities for in-game strategies and economy-building game hacks. The game will launch with 10,000 Crew NFTs and 10,000 Specialist NFTs will be added shortly after launch.
Halo Starter Pack
When you buy your first Planet or Asteroid NFT you will receive a free bonus pack to help start the game, this will include:
  • 1 x Free Starter Micro Shuttle Ship NFT
  • 1 x Free Halo Crew NFT (so you can start earning on missions)
  • Free Building: The Colony HQ building (so you can start earning daily token rewards from the start)
Each account is limited to one starter pack, so players with multiple Planets or Asteroids will not receive multiple NFTs.
Soft Stake NFTs for Rewards
Halo Crew and Specialist NFTs can be soft-staked for daily token rewards. This means that you can earn rewards from your NFTs without locking them up for months at a time — and still use them in the game as normal. To achieve this, the game simply reads your wallet address to verify your NFTs, then automatically starts generating daily rewards. The game contract does not actually interact with the NFTs in any way.

Halo, is a play-to-earn sci-fi strategy game that focuses heavily on providing its players with a plethora of different ways to earn through the ecosystem’s 15 in-game currencies and the game’s main cryptocurrency, Halo. The game will launch in Q4, 2024, on both the Ethereum and Polygon networks.
What is Halo?
Halo is a tokenized, sci-fi strategy game set in deep space, with a huge galaxy of realistic planets, asteroids, dyson spheres, ringworlds, ships, and space structures for players to fully immerse themselves in. The game offers players several key gameplay loops, including: staking, planetary development, building structures, resource generation, ship building, missions, exploration, colonization, combat, and in-game trading. Players can utilize up to 15 in-game currencies in a multitude of different ways to forge their own unique path to wealth amongst the stars.
Exploring the Game
The more ambitious Halo explorers can engage in deep space exploration, combative gameplay in Contested Space, whilst extensively upgrading bases and other structures.
More casual players looking to earn passively can trade all of the in-game currencies, along with a multitude of in-game items, NFTs, and earn an APY from numerous staking options.
All Halo ships, resources, structures, and buildings are created by the players, and can be used to forge expansive space empires, mine exotic resources, or take on space colonization missions. Players can earn, mine, trade, or stake Halo’s 15 in-game currencies through playing the game, or using the in-game DEX.
Ways To Earn
Halo players can choose from a multitude of way to earn, including:
  • Developing planets and earning a reliable income of the 15 different tokens from different buildings and structures
  • Taking on daring, time-sensitive space missions
  • Exploring Contested Space
  • Trading & developing planets, asteroids, and other structures to sell P2P
  • Soft staking supported external NFTs + specialist NFT collections
  • Trade planets, Asteroids, Ships, & Crew NFTs via Halo Nexus NFT Marketplace
  • Trade Halo on MetaMask or Uniswap [ETH & MATIC]
  • Trade up to 15 in-game currencies on Halo’s native DEX
  • The Halo player referral program
  • Staking all Halo currencies via on-chain liquidity pools
Tokenomics
The Halo team is focused on making sure there are enough resources for further development of the game over a long period of time, creating an environment for stability and steady growth for both the game and the Halo community.
For this reason, there will be no Halo token sale. Instead, 80% of the total supply is locked into the game for players to earn, through various gameplay loops. This emergent economic structure is designed to benefit active players to have a key role in deciding the fate of the game. Players who wish to progress instantly can get a boost by purchasing Halo from DEXs or in the game.
